China dog risks life to save owner from snake, gets bitten, recovers in stable condition

Caring canine steps in after venomous pit viper appears in garden, owner drives for hours to get hands on snakebite medicine

Fran Lu · in Beijing · April 10, 2026 · 2 min read
A dog in China saved its owner from a snake bite, prompting the man to kill the reptile in retaliation. The injured canine is in a stable condition. Photo: Douyin

A family dog nearly sacrificed its life protecting its owner from a venomous snake in Hebei province, in a story that has touched hearts across Chinese social media.

The owner, identified as Meng, relocated her family to the countryside in June alongside two Shiba Inu dogs: two-year-old Hetao and one-year-old Duoduo.

On the morning of August 29, Duoduo's frantic barking in the front garden alerted Meng to danger. Upon investigation with Hetao by her side, she discovered a snake approaching her.

"Hetao immediately darted forward to protect me."

The snake was later identified as a short-tailed Mamushi—a venomous pit viper native to the region. Hetao had been bitten, with visible swelling and body stiffening becoming evident almost immediately.

Meng rushed Hetao to the nearest veterinary clinic, where staff cleaned the wound but did not have snakebite antidote on hand. Her husband then drove for hours to Beijing to obtain the necessary medicine.

Back home, Meng maintained intensive care around the clock.

"I stayed up to feed her water every 30 minutes."

Within a week, Hetao had recovered enough to avoid immediate danger. Three weeks after the incident, the wound had fully scabbed over and the dog was back to her usual self.

Reflecting on the ordeal, Meng was emotional.

"She risked her life to save me. I did everything to save her."

She advised other pet owners to keep their animals away from forests and grasslands following rainfall, when snakes are most likely to emerge.

The story went viral on Chinese social media, with one commenter summing up the feeling shared by many: "I would spend all my money to save my pet."
